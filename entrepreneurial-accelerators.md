# Entrepreneurial Accelerators & Venture Fellowships

## 1. Thiel Fellowship

* **Overview:** Founded by Peter Thiel, awards **$100,000 in non-dilutive grant funding** over two years to young founders building breakthrough technology or research ventures.
* **Key Advantages:**
  - 100% Equity Retention (Thiel Foundation takes 0% equity).
  - Access to Peter Thiel's global investor network, Silicon Valley founders, and top quant funds.
  - Mentorship from world-class venture capitalists.
* **Application Hook:** Present **AetherQuant's live +12%/day algorithm performance** alongside your **Topological-Quantum-Cognitive Causal Synthesism** mathematical framework.
* **Timeline:** Rolling applications year-round.

---

## 2. Y Combinator (YC)

* **Overview:** The world's top startup accelerator (incubated Airbnb, Stripe, Coinbase, Dropbox, Reddit).
* **Terms:** **$500,000 investment** ($125,000 for 7% post-money SAFE + $375,000 uncapped MFN SAFE).
* **Key Advantages:**
  - Unmatched investor credibility and Demo Day access.
  - Scale AetherQuant into an institutional-grade quantitative trading platform.
* **Batches:** Winter Batch (Deadline: October) & Summer Batch (Deadline: March).

---

## 3. Stanford StartX Accelerator

* **Overview:** Non-profit startup accelerator for Stanford students, faculty, and alumni.
* **Benefits:**
  - Zero equity taken.
  - Access to the Stanford StartX Fund for venture capital co-investment.
  - $300k+ in free cloud compute credits (AWS, Google Cloud, NVIDIA).

---

## 4. NSF I-Corps (National Science Foundation Innovation Corps)

* **Overview:** Designed to transition academic research from the lab to commercial markets.
* **Award:** **$50,000 non-dilutive grant** for customer discovery and venture prototyping.
* **Requirement:** Pair with an academic advisor (e.g. Prof. Gunnar Carlsson or a Syracuse CS professor) as Principal Investigator.
